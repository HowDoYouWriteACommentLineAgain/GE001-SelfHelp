Everybody wants to rule the world

- Everybody wants to rule the world by Tears for Fears, 1985

Back in my highschool days we used to joke that there were three stages in a class projects. There was the first stage where everyone would suggest things but never really do anything. Then once enough time has past, the leader, or the poor soul that was singled out by the group, would start to assign tasks. and then finally, 48 hours before the deadline. The elected leader suddenly turns into the most autocratic dicatator putting Hassan Sudein, Kim Jong Un and Gadaffi to Shame.

Don't really have much to say about this. As someone who has experienced being a group leader, either by being forcefully thrust in to the position, or through tactical alliances where we agreed to share the burden of leading, (in the end I had to step in as a leader full time), This really boils down to what works with you. If you are lucky and your group mates are competent or rather, far exceed your capabilities, you can just watch rather than breathing on their neck on whether or not they have completed their task. But be prepared for those with deliquencies in their tasks. Have contingency in place in case they didn't do anything.

Lazy ants theory

DID YOU KNOW... that within an ant colony, about 20 percent are always lazy? Even if you eliminate the 20 percent of ants within the colony, the population of lazy ants will come back to that level. It seems that lazyness is an inherent trait within a group of animals. There will always be people who are lazy in general even if you eliminate them, that only produces a new set of lazy people within the group.

Source: I found this out from a Manga
but I also found a study from the internet here:
Discussions of worker ants’ rule-based CHC dealing with changing environments by Akimoto Kamiya, Kazuya Abiko, Shigenobu Kobayashi

By the time I entered College, the number of people who are actually responsible have increased. It seems the people in highschool, those who don't do anything and expects to get credit, have either decided not to go to College or are doing something else. Not to say that they don't exist in college. But most of the time, people have actual good reason to not help and Mercy is a quality not strained. If you are a leader, then act like it. Leaders don't threaten those who can't but help them as best they could, in a way that benefits the group.

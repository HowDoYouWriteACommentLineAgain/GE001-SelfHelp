Could I interest you in everything? All of the time? Apathy's a tragedy,
And boredom is a crime, Anything and everything, All of the time

- Welcome to the Internet by Bo Burnham, 2021

As chonically online person, I can tell you for certain that the internet changes a person. I have seen first hand how a person can change whether they are on the internet or not. One moment they are a keyboard warrior online and when they're in real life they become a cinammon bun.

Anonimity can be a powerful tool in the internet but it can also lead to a hazardous mindset where normal etiquette and conduct is forgotten once you go online, as your identity is masked behind a profile pic and a username.

We have developed speed, but we have shut ourselves in
-Charlie Chaplin

In this digital age where we are both so connected with eachother and yet so distant at the same time we often forget ourselves when we go online. We often take on a facade or rather become someone we are if we weren't burdened by social norms. Sometimes this leads to distasteful behavior. And if you do it enough times, it can lead to that behavior leaking in the real world as you start to unravel the border seperating your online self and your real world self.

Ask yourself this, Does your online self reflect who you are as a person. Who is real and who is the facade? Your online self or your real self?
